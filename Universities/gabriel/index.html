Brigam Yong University Idaho

History:

Brigham Young University–Idaho is a private college in Rexburg, Idaho. Founded 138 years ago in 1888, the college is owned and operated by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Previously known as Ricks College, it transitioned from a junior college to a baccalaureate institution in 2001.

Number of programs:

BYU-Idaho offers over 100 bachelor's degree programs and over 20 associate degree programs, along with numerous online certificates and degrees, focusing on undergraduate education. Organized into 35 departments, the school provides diverse academic options across six colleges.
